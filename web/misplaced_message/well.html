Wells

Wells serve as vital access points to the vast, hidden aquifers that store the majority of the planet's liquid freshwater within layers of rock and sediment. Throughout history, these vertical shafts acted as the primary anchors for permanent settlements, allowing ancient civilizations to thrive in arid regions far from the banks of natural rivers. Their strategic importance made them high-value targets during wartime, as controlling or poisoning a community's primary well was a common and devastating method of forcing a surrender.
