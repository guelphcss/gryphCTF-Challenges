The Plains

Plains are vast, flat expanses of land that provide unparalleled strategic visibility and maneuverability, making them the historical site of decisive large-scale military engagements and cavalry charges. Their deep, unobstructed soils and predictable terrain transformed these regions into the world's breadbaskets, fueling the rise of massive agrarian civilizations through industrial-scale farming. Because they lack natural barriers like mountains or dense forests, plains have served as high-speed corridors for migration, trade, and the rapid expansion of empires throughout human history.
